Project 3 The Life Of Ruby Bridges

This interactive narative I have produced is based on a true story about a girl called Ruby bridges who was one of the first black students to attend integrated schools in New Orleans. The aim of the interactive narrative is to produce secondary and post 16 educational resources for BBC Bitesize to educate young black girls thta feel like they can't fit in dominaly white areas. I want this story book to give people the same courage as Ruby had as well as educate them about her experience of going to William Frantz Elementary School. 

In my opinion, I think that page shows off my strengths the most because you can see that the American flag conveys the change in American history (the first black to attend an all-white school). In terms of editing the American flag you can see that I am familiar with using photoshops tools such as the rectangle tool to create the rectangles and the custom shape tool to create the stars. I was also able to experiment with the layers changing the opacity and changing the layer to a dissolve. Another reason that I think this page stands out is because of the quote included by Rubys' mother because it speaks volumes. She told Ruby to hold her head up high, precisely what she did. 

Photograghy editing process

I opened up a new project on Photopea and used the rectangle tool to draw a rectangle in the top left hand corner. I then double clicked on shape 1 and changed the colour of the rectangle to navy blue.

After that I drew rectangles with the rectangle tool around the blue rectangle and then double clicked on the shape and changed the colour either to white or red.

With the custom shape tool I drew eight stars into the blue rectangle. I then went onto “File” and exported this as a PNG.

In a new project I changed this layer to a dissolve and changed the opacity to 80% to give the flag this sparkly effect.

Sound Effect Practical Experiment 

In order to create my own sound effects for my interactive narrative I had to do complete some research. 

Rain 

How to create a rain storm with your hands

This youtube video taught me that you can create a rain sound effect by having one person clicking their fingers, one perosn rubbing their hands together and one person tapping my knees. This didn't end up working for me so instead o got one person to tap their fingers on a cardboard box and a I got another person to click their fingers. This resulted in being more successful as it sounded more natural. 

Bones cracking 

How to create a sound effect of bones breaking by using everyday household items

I created this sound effect by taking a bunch of celery and holding the stalks together and then snapping them in half.

Heartbeat 

How to create a heartbeat sound effect

This YouTube video showed me that you can create this sound effect using a large plastic trash can. The plastic popping in and out can create two distinct in/out “pumping” sounds. I didn't have a plastic trash so I came up with an idea to used a jam lid as an alternative. This worked out to work out fine as I was able to push the lid in and out to create the same distinct pumping sounds.
